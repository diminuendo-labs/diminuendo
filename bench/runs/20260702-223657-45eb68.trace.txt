I used a stack to keep track of the opening parentheses. As I iterate through the string, every time I encounter an opening parenthesis '(', I push it onto the stack. For every closing parenthesis ')', if the stack is empty or the top of the stack is not an opening parenthesis, the string is unbalanced, and I return `False`. Otherwise, I pop the top of the stack. After iterating through the entire string, if the stack is empty, all parentheses were matched correctly, and I return `True`; otherwise, I return `False`. This approach ensures that every closing parenthesis has a matching opening one in the correct order.
