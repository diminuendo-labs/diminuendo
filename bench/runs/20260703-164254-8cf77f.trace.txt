In this solution, I utilized Python's built-in `groupby()` function to group consecutive whitespaces and words together. The generator expression `lambda x: x.isspace()` is used in the groupby function to group spaces and non-spaces separately. Then, for each group, a conditional expression checks whether its first character (accessed via slice `w[0]`) is an alphabetic character using `c.isalpha()`. If it is, the word gets title-cased; otherwise, it remains unchanged. Finally, the `join()` function concatenates all the words with a single space between them. This approach ensures that the edge case where entirely uppercase words remain as they are is handled correctly.
