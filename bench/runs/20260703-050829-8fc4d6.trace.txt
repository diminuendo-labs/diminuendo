I started by sorting the list to handle consecutive numbers easily. I then iterated through the sorted list, keeping track of the current range with `start` and `end`. When a non-consecutive number is encountered, I decided whether to add the single number or the range to the result based on whether `start` equals `end`. This approach ensures that all consecutive numbers are collapsed into ranges, while single numbers remain as is. The edge case of an empty list is handled by returning an empty string immediately.
