**

The function `kth_ordered` first checks for an empty list or a non-positive `k`, returning `None` in those cases. It then sorts the words based on their length and, for words of equal length, reverses the lexicographical order by using the negative ASCII value of the first character. This ensures that shorter words come first and among words of equal length, the one starting with a later alphabet letter comes first. Finally, it checks if `k` is within the bounds of the sorted list and returns the `k-1` index word (since Python indexing starts at 0).
