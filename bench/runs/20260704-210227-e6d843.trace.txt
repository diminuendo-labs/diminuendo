To accomplish the task, I first handle empty and None inputs by returning None. Then, I remove both the minimum and maximum values from the list. After that, I sort the remaining numbers, find the middle index, calculate the sum of the numbers around the middle index, and divide it by the length of the original list. This gives us the trimmed mean. The edge case considered here is when the number of elements in the list is odd as in that case, the mid-index would be either an integer or a float, but the sum calculation ensures that it still produces accurate results.
