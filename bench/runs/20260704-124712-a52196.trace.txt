I used the `split()` function to separate the string by commas and semicolons. However, this approach would yield multiple empty pieces at the start and end if there are consecutive delimiters. I handled that by stripping the string and dropping empty pieces before splitting.
To collapse runs of consecutive delimiters (mixed or not), I first combined all pieces with a single comma separator and then used regular expressions to find runs of consecutive delimiters. Finally, I iterated through the pieces and collapsed each one that matches a run of delimiters until no such piece is found anymore. The edge case I handled here is when there are multiple runs of consecutive delimiters separated by non-delimiter elements.
