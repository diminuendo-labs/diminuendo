To solve this task, I first converted the input string to lowercase and split it into words using whitespace as a separator. Then, I created a dictionary where keys are unique words and values are their respective counts in the input string. Next, I found the word with the maximum count using the built-in `max()` function.

In case of ties, I checked if any characters in the tied words were alphanumeric (indicating that it might not be a real English word), and stripped out non-alphabetic characters from such words to ensure a correct result. The edge case mentioned was handled by checking for words with numbers or special characters while finding the most common word.
