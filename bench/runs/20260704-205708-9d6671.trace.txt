To implement the trimmed mean function, I first checked if the input list is empty or has fewer than three elements, and in such cases returned None to adhere to the stated edge case. Then, I sorted the list and calculated the indices of the minimum and maximum values while making sure not to go out of bounds when the list is too small. After that, I summed up the middle elements (excluding the minimum and maximum) and divided by the number of middle elements (plus one for the initial min_idx) to calculate the trimmed mean.
