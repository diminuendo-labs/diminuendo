This ensures that when two touching ranges are adjacent in the sorted list, they can be easily merged.

The algorithm then iterates through the sorted pairs, merging them with the last range in the `merged` list if they overlap or touch, otherwise appending the new range to the `merged` list. This approach is efficient and concise, leveraging Python's built-in sorting and list operations. The edge case of an empty input list is handled by returning an empty list immediately.
