I chose to use a stack data structure to keep track of opening parentheses. As I iterate through the input string, when I encounter an opening parenthesis I push it onto the stack. When I encounter a closing parenthesis, I check if the stack is empty (which would mean there's no matching opening parenthesis), and if not, I pop the top element from the stack. At the end of the function, I return whether the stack is empty (which means all parentheses were matched) or not (meaning some were left unmatched). This approach ensures that parentheses are balanced without explicitly keeping track of their positions in the string.
