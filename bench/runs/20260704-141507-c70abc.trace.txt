I used Python's `re.split` function with a regular expression that matches one or more commas or semicolons to handle multiple consecutive delimiters. The `strip()` method is called on the input string to remove leading and trailing whitespace, which ensures empty pieces at the start and end are dropped. Comprehension is used to filter out any empty strings resulting from splits. Finally, `''.join(pieces)` combines the non-empty pieces with a single comma between each. This approach handles the edge case where delimiters are consecutive or mixed without introducing additional commas.
