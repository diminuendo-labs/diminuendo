**

The function `trimmed_mean` calculates the mean of a list after removing one occurrence of the minimum and one occurrence of the maximum value. If the input list is empty, it returns `None`. The approach involves:

1. Sorting the list to easily identify and remove the minimum and maximum values.
2. Summing up the remaining elements (excluding the first and last elements).
3. Calculating the mean by dividing the sum by the count of remaining elements.

This method ensures that exactly one occurrence of each extreme value is removed, and it handles empty lists as specified.
