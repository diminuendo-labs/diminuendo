The function `kth_ordered` is designed to handle a list of words and an integer `k`. It first checks if `k` is within a valid range (greater than 0 and less than or equal to the length of the words list). If not, it returns `None`. Then, it sorts the words primarily by their length in ascending order. For words of equal length, it uses a secondary sorting criterion that sorts them reverse-alphabetically by taking the ASCII value of the last character (negated to achieve reverse sorting). Finally, it returns the k-th word from the sorted list, adjusting for zero-based indexing by subtracting 1 from `k`.
