To implement the trimmed mean function, I first ensured that the function returns None if the input list is empty. Then I sorted the list and found the middle index of the non-extremum values. To handle the edge case where the minimum or maximum appears multiple times at the start or end of the list, I used a while loop to find the indices of the minimum and maximum occurrences that are not at the beginning or end of the sorted list. Finally, I calculated the mean by summing all non-extremum values excluding the two dropped minimums and maximums, and dividing by (n-2), where n is the total number of elements in the list.
