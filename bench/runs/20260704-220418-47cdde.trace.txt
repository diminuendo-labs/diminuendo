In this solution, I first handle the edge case of an empty list by returning `None`. Then, I sort the input list to ensure the minimum and maximum values are at the beginning and end. After that, I calculate the trimmed mean by summing the values from the second smallest to the second largest index (excluding) and dividing it by the number of elements being averaged (which is `length - 2`). Finally, the function returns the calculated trimmed mean as a float.
