** The function `kth_ordered` first checks for edge cases where the input list is empty or if `k` is out of range. It then sorts the words based on two criteria using Python's built-in sorting functionality with a custom key. The primary criterion is the negative length of the word (to sort by increasing length), and the secondary criterion is the reverse alphabetical order of the words (achieved by reversing each word before comparison). Finally, it returns the `k`-th element from the sorted list, adjusting for zero-based indexing by subtracting 1 from `k`.
