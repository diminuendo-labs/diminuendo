I started by sorting the list of ranges based on their start values to ensure that any potential overlaps or touches are adjacent. Then, I iterated through the sorted list while maintaining a list of merged ranges. For each range, I checked if it overlapped with or touched the last range in my merged list. If it did, I updated the end of the last merged range to be the maximum of its current end and the current range's end. If it didn't overlap or touch, I simply appended the current range to the merged list. This approach ensures that all overlapping or touching ranges are merged into a single range, and the result is sorted by start value.
