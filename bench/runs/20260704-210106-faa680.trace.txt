First, I checked if the list is empty and returned `None` in such case to handle an edge case. Next, I sorted the list using the built-in `sort()` function, then removed the first (minimum) and last (maximum) elements while keeping at least one occurrence of both. Lastly, I calculated the mean by summing up the remaining elements and dividing it by their count. The result is returned as a float. This solution ensures conciseness and readability.
