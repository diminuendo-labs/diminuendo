I chose to split the input string by underscores ('_'), then iterate through each word, making the first letter uppercase and joining them back together using an underscore. This approach ensures that only the first letter of the first word is capitalized, while the rest of the words are in lower case. I also included a condition to check if the word exists (i.e., it's not an empty string), ensuring that leading, trailing and repeated underscores are dropped.

One edge case this function handles is converting identifiers such as '__foo_bar' into 'FooBar', where the initial double underscore is preserved while leading and trailing underscores are dropped.
