To implement the `kth_ordered` function, I first checked for edge cases such as an empty list of words or a non-positive value of `k`, returning `None` in these scenarios. The main logic involves sorting the words based on two criteria: increasing length and reverse-alphabetical order for words of equal length. This is achieved using Python's built-in `sorted` function with a custom key. Finally, I returned the `k-th` word from the sorted list, adjusting for zero-based indexing by subtracting one from `k`.
