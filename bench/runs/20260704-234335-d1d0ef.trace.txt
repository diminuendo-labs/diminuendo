I first checked for the edge case where `words` is empty or `k` is out of range, returning `None` in those cases. To sort the words by increasing length and reverse-alphabetically among equal-length words, I used Python's `sorted()` function with a custom key. The lambda function sorts primarily by word length and secondarily by the ASCII value of the last character in reverse order (achieved by prefixing `-ord(x[-1])`). Finally, I returned the k-th word from the sorted list, adjusting for zero-based indexing by using `k-1`.
