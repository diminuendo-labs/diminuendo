I chose to sort the words using Python's built-in `sorted` function with a custom key. The key is a lambda function that returns a tuple where the first element is the length of the word (for sorting by increasing length) and the second element is the negative ASCII value of the last character of the word (for reverse-alphabetical order). This approach ensures that words are sorted primarily by their length in ascending order, and for words of equal length, they are ordered reverse-alphabetically. The function also handles the edge case where `k` is out of range by returning `None`.
