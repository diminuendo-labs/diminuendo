I used a sorting approach to first order the list of numbers from smallest to largest. Then I determined the median of the sorted list, as it handles an odd-length list correctly by taking the average of the middle two elements. After that, I computed the sum of all numbers in the list that are greater than the median but less than the maximum value (to ensure only one minimum is dropped). Finally, I divided this sum by the number of remaining items minus 2 to get the trimmed mean. An edge case handled here is when the input list is empty, returning `None` in such a scenario.
