I utilized Python's built-in sorting function to arrange the list in ascending order. Then, I first checked if the list is empty and returned None accordingly. For lists with less than two elements, I simply calculated the arithmetic mean of the list. For lists with more than one element, I found the median by indexing into the sorted list and calculated the trimmed mean by excluding the minimum and maximum values (and their occurrences) and averaging the remaining values. The edge case I handled is when the input list contains only one or zero elements.
