I first checked if the input list is empty, and if so, returned None. Then I sorted the list and computed the median index. If the length of the list is less than or equal to 2, I returned the mean of all elements except the first and last ones. For larger lists, I found the indices of the minimum and maximum occurring more than once by finding the last occurrence of the minimum after the median and the first occurrence of the maximum before the median. The function then computes and returns the mean of all elements between these two indices. This approach handles an edge case where the list contains duplicates at the ends, ensuring that only one occurrence of the minimum and maximum is excluded when computing the trimmed mean.
