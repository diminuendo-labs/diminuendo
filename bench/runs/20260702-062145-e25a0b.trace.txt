I used a simple iterative approach to solve this problem. I maintained a stack of opening parentheses and iterated over each character in the string. When I encountered an opening parenthesis, it was pushed onto the stack. When I encountered a closing parenthesis, the top element on the stack (if any) was popped. If there were no opening parentheses on the stack when a closing one was encountered, the function returned False (edge case: empty string). Finally, after iterating over all characters, if the stack was empty, it meant that every opening parenthesis had been closed and I returned True.
