First, I sorted the list `nums` and handled the edge case when the list is empty by returning None. For lists with less than three elements, I calculated the mean of the remaining elements, excluding the minimum and maximum values, as there's only one occurrence of each in such cases. In longer lists, I calculated the average of the middle two numbers (avoiding both the minimum and maximum) to get the trimmed mean. This approach ensures that the function is correct, concise, and readable while handling an edge case where the list has less than three elements.
