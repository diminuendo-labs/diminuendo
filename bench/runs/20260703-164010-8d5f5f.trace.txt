In this implementation, I used a while loop to iterate through the sequence defined by `start`, `stop`, and `step`. The loop continues until the current value (`i`) is greater than or equal to `stop`. Each time through the loop, we append the current value to the result list and then increment `i` by `step`. I also made sure to handle edge cases when `step` is zero or negative, and when `start` is at or past `stop`.
