I chose to implement the "concept" approach that handles ties by returning the index of its last occurrence. This is particularly fitting for this task as we're looking for the longest whitespace-separated word in a string, and if there's a tie, we should return the last such word. I did this by iterating over the list of words from right to left (i.e., `range(len(words) - 1, -1, -1)`), which ensures that when we find a longer word, it will be the most recent one and therefore the "last" in the case of a tie. This implementation is also concise and readable, as required by the task.
