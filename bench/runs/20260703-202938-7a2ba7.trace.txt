To find the second largest distinct value in a list, I first ensure there are at least two distinct values using the len(set()) function. Then, I sort the unique numbers in descending order and use a pair of variables (max1, max2) to keep track of the two largest distinct values found so far. The outer loop iterates through the sorted list, updating `max1` with larger numbers it encounters initially, and setting `max2` only if the number is larger than `max1` but not equal to it (to avoid overwriting `max1` with a smaller second-largest value). The edge case I handled here is when there are fewer than two distinct values in the input list.
