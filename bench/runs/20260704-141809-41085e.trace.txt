**: I used Python's `re.split()` function with the pattern `',|;'` to split the string on both commas and semicolons. This handles multiple consecutive delimiters as a single split. Then, I used a list comprehension to strip any leading or trailing whitespace from each piece and filter out empty strings. Finally, I joined the cleaned pieces back together with a comma. The edge case of leading or trailing multiple delimiters is handled by the `strip()` method within the list comprehension, which ensures that no empty strings are included in the final result.
