First, I handled the edge case where the input is an empty list or contains less than 3 elements by returning None. Then, I sorted the numbers in ascending order and calculated the number of occurrences for both minimum and maximum values. If there are multiple occurrences, I removed one occurrence each from minimum and maximum values. Finally, I computed the mean of the remaining numbers. This approach ensures that the function is correct, concise, readable, and handles the mentioned edge case.
