First, I sorted the list and determined the middle index. Then, I found the indices of the minimum and maximum values in the sorted list by iterating through the list until we find a non-repeated occurrence of the min or max value. After that, I created a new list with the trimmed numbers, calculated the sum of the trimmed numbers, and finally divided it by the number of elements in the trimmed list to obtain the trimmed mean as a float. The edge case handled is when the input list is empty, returning None instead of an error.
