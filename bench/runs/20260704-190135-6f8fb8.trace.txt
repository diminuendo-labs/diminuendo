The function `kth_ordered` first checks if the input list is empty or if `k` is less than or equal to 0, returning `None` in these cases as they are considered out of range. It then groups words by their length and sorts each group reverse-alphabetically. After that, it flattens the dictionary into a single sorted list by word length. Finally, it checks if `k` is within the bounds of the list and returns the k-th word (adjusted for zero-indexing).
